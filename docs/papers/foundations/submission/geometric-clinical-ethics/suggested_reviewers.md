# Suggested Reviewers for Journal of Medical Ethics

Enter these when submitting via ScholarOne. All are independent of the author and from different institutions/countries.

## 1. Daniel P. Sulmasy
- **Affiliation:** Georgetown University, Washington, DC, USA
- **Degree:** MD, PhD (Philosophy)
- **Email:** daniel.sulmasy@georgetown.edu
- **Expertise:** Clinical ethics, moral distress, dignity in medicine, end-of-life ethics, philosophy of medicine
- **Justification:** Professor Sulmasy is one of the leading physician-ethicists, with foundational work on dignity and the intrinsic worth of persons in medical contexts. His dual training in medicine and philosophy positions him uniquely to evaluate whether the nine-dimensional clinical decision complex captures the moral structure of clinical reasoning. His work on the biopsychosocial-spiritual model of care engages directly with the paper's claim that scalar outcome metrics miss morally relevant dimensions. He can assess both the mathematical formalism and its clinical plausibility.

## 2. Angus Dawson
- **Affiliation:** University of Sydney, Sydney, Australia
- **Degree:** PhD in Philosophy, University of Liverpool
- **Email:** angus.dawson@sydney.edu.au
- **Expertise:** Public health ethics, resource allocation, COVID-19 ethics, triage, justice in healthcare
- **Justification:** Professor Dawson is Editor-in-Chief of *Public Health Ethics* and has published extensively on resource allocation during pandemics, triage ethics, and justice in health systems. He is ideally positioned to evaluate the paper's treatment of triage as constrained multi-agent pathfinding, the Divergence Theorem (utilitarian vs. manifold-optimal triage), and the COVID-19 moral injury predictions. His experience with public health ethics frameworks enables him to assess whether the geometric approach adds genuine value beyond existing consequentialist and deontological frameworks.

## 3. Cynda Hylton Rushton
- **Affiliation:** Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, MD, USA
- **Degree:** PhD, MSN, RN, FAAN
- **Email:** crushto1@jhu.edu
- **Expertise:** Moral distress, moral resilience, moral injury in healthcare, clinician well-being, nursing ethics
- **Justification:** Professor Rushton is the foremost researcher on moral resilience and moral distress in healthcare professionals. Her monograph *Moral Resilience* (Oxford, 2018)---cited in the manuscript---defines the field. She is uniquely qualified to evaluate whether the mathematical characterization of moral injury as forced boundary crossing (structurally distinct from burnout) is consistent with the empirical literature on moral distress, moral residue, and the crescendo effect, and whether the Moral Injury Index is a viable predictive tool.

## 6. Wendy Dean
- **Affiliation:** Moral Injury of Healthcare, Carlisle, PA, USA
- **Degree:** MD (Psychiatry)
- **Email:** wendy@fixmoralinjury.org
- **Expertise:** Moral injury in healthcare (coined the term for healthcare context), clinician well-being, systems-level ethical failures, healthcare policy
